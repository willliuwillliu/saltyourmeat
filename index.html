    About

    I read Salt Fat Acid Heat Salt Fat Acid Heat about a year ago, and it completely changed my cooking. If you're a beginner-intermediate cook, I highly recommend you check it out. The chapter on salt was the most important one for me, as it taught me to not be afraid of salt, and how to think about using it. The key takeaway from that chapter was the importance of dry brining (just a fancy way of saying salt) meat well in advance. I've always salted my food right before and while cooking it, but I was blown away by the effect that dry brining had on my steaks. It was such a game changer for me, that *not* salting a bird a day in advance has become kitchen taboo. You can read more about dry brining here.

    This meat salting/dry brining calculator is inspired by Samin's salting guidelines in Salt Fat Acid Heat. In the same spirit as her book, I hope this website helps encourage people to salt in advance, and helps people feel comfortable with the amount of salt they should use. It's much easier to adjust salt in a soup or pasta sauce, but it's much harder to get it right on a roast that you can't taste until it comes out of the oven!
